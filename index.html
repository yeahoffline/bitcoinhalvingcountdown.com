What is the Bitcoin Halving?

Every 210,000 blocks (approximately every 4 years), the Bitcoin block reward is cut in half. This event reduces the rate at which new bitcoins are created, making Bitcoin increasingly scarce over time. The halving is a fundamental part of Bitcoin's deflationary monetary policy, ensuring a maximum supply of 21 million coins.

Why does Bitcoin halve every 210,000 blocks?

Bitcoin’s code hard-caps supply at 21 million coins by cutting the block subsidy in half every 210,000 blocks, or roughly four years. This predictable schedule slows the issuance rate and enforces Bitcoin’s deflationary monetary policy.

How accurate is the Bitcoin halving countdown date?

BitcoinHalvingCountdown.com constantly recalculates the ETA using the trailing average block time. Because actual block production varies, the countdown is expressed as an estimate that tightens as we approach the halving block.

What happened during past Bitcoin halvings?

Previous halvings in 2012, 2016, 2020, and 2024 immediately cut miner rewards and gradually reduced BTC inflation. Historically, Bitcoin entered multi-month accumulation phases followed by new all-time highs, although past performance does not guarantee future results.
